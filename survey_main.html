Policy Survey

Health at a Glance: Latin America and the Caribbean

Thematic chapter on quality-of-care policies

This questionnaire is designed to collect information from LAC countries on quality-of-care policies and overall government efforts to assure and improve the quality of healthcare. The work based on this information will be released in 2026 as a thematic chapter of the third edition of Health at a Glance: Latin America and the Caribbean, co-published by the OECD, the World Bank and the Inter-American Development Bank.

The questionnaire has two parts. Part I maps the state of play in quality-of-care policies—what exists in law and governance, the standards that guide practice, how performance is measured and improved, and how patients and professionals are engaged. Part II asks about the digital health capabilities and the uses of health data for quality improvement.

Definition of key terms used in this questionnaire

  • Hospital: Establishments primarily engaged in providing medical, diagnostic, and treatment services that include physician, nursing, and other health services to in‑patients and the specialised accommodation services required by in‑patients. Hospitals may also provide out‑patient services as a secondary activity.
  • Primary Health Care (PHC): Primary care is the first level of contact of individuals, the family and community with the (national) health system bringing health care as close as possible to where people live and work and provides continuing and comprehensive medical care to individuals and families in an ambulatory setting. It may be provided by general practitioners (or "family doctors") and their teams but could also be provided by out‑patient departments of first level hospitals.
  • Health care quality: Healthcare quality comprises three core dimensions of care—effectiveness, safety and patient‑centredness (responsiveness). These dimensions are applied across the care pathway: preventive, acute, chronic, and palliative care.
